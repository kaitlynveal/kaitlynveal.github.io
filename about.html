About Me

I am a Peace Corps Volunteer in Guatemala serving as a Rural Extension Volunteer with a focus on food security, nutrition, and climate change resilience.

I hold a B.A. in Environmental Science and Policy with a minor in Geography from California State University, Long Beach. During my studies, I developed a foundation in environmental systems, spatial thinking, and policy analysis, along with applied experience in Geographic Information Systems and remote sensing (GEOG 473).

In my current role as a volunteer, I work with rural farmers and women’s groups to support agricultural production, nutrition initiatives, and income-generating projects. Recent climate variability associated with El Niño has significantly impacted crop production in my region, with excessive rainfall affecting planting cycles, yields, and food availability.

These experiences have highlighted the interconnected challenges of climate change, agricultural vulnerability, and food insecurity. I have also observed how access and affordability shape nutrition outcomes, where nutrient-poor foods are often more accessible than healthier alternatives in rural communities.

These field experiences have strengthened my interest in geographic information systems as a tool for analyzing spatial patterns in food systems, climate impacts, and rural development challenges. I am particularly interested in how GIS and remote sensing can support data-driven decision-making in resource-limited and climate-affected regions.

I am seeking to pursue graduate study in Geographic Information Systems to deepen my technical skills and apply spatial analysis to challenges in food security, climate adaptation, and sustainable rural development.
