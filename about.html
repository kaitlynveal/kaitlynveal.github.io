About Me

I am a Peace Corps Volunteer in Guatemala working in rural extension with a focus on food security and nutrition. My field experience has strengthened my interest in understanding how environmental conditions, agriculture, and access to resources shape community wellbeing.

I hold a B.A. in Environmental Science and Policy with a minor in Geography from California State University, Long Beach. During my studies, I developed a strong foundation in environmental systems, spatial thinking, and policy analysis.

I have coursework and applied experience in Geographic Information Systems, including ArcGIS-based mapping and spatial analysis. I also completed coursework in remote sensing (GEOG 473), where I learned how satellite imagery and geospatial data can be used to analyze environmental change and land use patterns.

My professional interests lie in applying geospatial tools and analysis to challenges in food security, public health, rural development, and climate change impacts. I am particularly interested in how GIS and remote sensing can support evidence-based decision-making in resource-limited settings, especially as communities adapt to increasing environmental and climate-related pressures.
