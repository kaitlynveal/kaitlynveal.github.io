About Me

Peace Corps Volunteer | Food Security, Climate Resilience & GIS Interests

Focus Areas: Food Security • Climate Change • Rural Development • GIS & Remote Sensing

I am a Peace Corps Volunteer in Guatemala serving as a Rural Extension Volunteer focused on food security, nutrition, and climate change resilience.

I hold a B.A. in Environmental Science and Policy with a minor in Geography from California State University, Long Beach. My academic background includes geographic information systems, remote sensing, and environmental policy analysis.

In my field work, I support rural farmers and women’s groups on agricultural development projects that address food production, nutrition, and income generation. Climate variability, particularly El Niño-related heavy rainfall, has significantly impacted agricultural systems and crop stability in the communities where I work.

These experiences have strengthened my interest in using GIS and spatial analysis to understand patterns of food insecurity, climate impacts, and rural vulnerability.

I am seeking to pursue graduate study in Geographic Information Systems to deepen my technical skills and apply spatial analysis to real-world challenges in food security and climate adaptation.
