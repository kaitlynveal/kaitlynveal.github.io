About Me

Peace Corps Volunteer | Food Security • Climate Change • GIS & Remote Sensing

Focus Areas

Food Security • Nutrition • Rural Development • Climate Change Adaptation • GIS • Remote Sensing

Background

I am a Peace Corps Volunteer in Guatemala serving as a Rural Extension Volunteer. My work focuses on food security, nutrition, and climate resilience in rural communities.

Education

Bachelor of Arts in Environmental Science and Policy
Minor in Geography
California State University, Long Beach

Field Experience

I work directly with farmers and women’s groups on agricultural development projects that support food production, nutrition, and household income generation.

In my current work, climate variability linked to El Niño has significantly impacted agricultural systems, with heavy rainfall affecting planting cycles, crop yields, and food availability.

I also observe how food insecurity is shaped not only by production challenges but also by access and affordability, where nutrient-poor foods are often more available than healthier options.

GIS & Remote Sensing Interest

I am interested in applying geographic information systems and remote sensing to analyze spatial patterns in food insecurity, climate impacts, and rural development challenges.

My goal is to use spatial analysis to better understand vulnerability and support data-driven decision-making in development and environmental contexts.
